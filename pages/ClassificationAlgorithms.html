A Comparative Analysis of Classification Algorithms for Land Cover Mapping: A Case Study of Ruaka, Kiambu County

Key Methodology

  • Preprocessing: Filtering, compositing, and cloud masking were applied in Google Earth Engine (GEE) to enhance classification accuracy.
  • Classification & Validation: Land cover classes (Built-up, Agriculture, Vegetation, and Bareland) were mapped using CART, RF, and SVM, validated using Overall Accuracy (OA) and Kappa Coefficient metrics.
  • Comparison & Analysis: Performance of classifiers was compared in detecting land cover changes over time.

Results & Findings

  • Accuracy: Random Forest consistently performed best, with the highest OA (96%) and Kappa (0.95) in 2023, followed by SVM, and then CART.
  • Land Cover Trends: Urban areas expanded, agriculture declined, and forest cover fluctuated, indicating rapid urbanization in Ruaka.
  • Algorithm Suitability: RF was the most reliable classifier across all years, while SVM performed better in detecting vegetation changes.

Conclusion & Recommendations

The study underscores Random Forest as the most effective classifier for mixed land use areas like Ruaka. It also highlights the need for ancillary data (e.g., soil type, elevation) and improved classification techniques for bareland detection. These findings support urban planning and environmental monitoring efforts in rapidly changing landscapes.
